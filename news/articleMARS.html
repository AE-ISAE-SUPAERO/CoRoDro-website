M.A.R.S club

discover the M.A.R.S club from ISAE-Supaero
February 22, 2021

Martin Badoil

The Mars Analog Research and Simulation Club (M.A.R.S) brings together around thirty students who are passionate or curious about space. Founded in 2014, it allows students to develop their skills thanks to three very different projects.

The first one is the sciences vulgarization, groups of three to five students visit middle and high school students to make them discover the wonders of space. These presentations are made to measure, correspond to the level of the students and aims to awaken their scientific interest. This opportunity allows young students, who are often very curious, to learn more about space, planets or rockets and to ask any questions they may have. This project of scientific vulgarization is also carried by OSE ISAE, the social association of ISAE Supaero university.

The M.A.R.S club also has the opportunity to send seven students to the Mars Desert Research Station (MDRS). This Martian life simulation station located in the desert of Utah is an ideal place to carry out several experiments that they have been preparing for months beforehand. The students have the opportunity to don astronaut suits and produce numerous reports on scientific and social tests they have carried out at the station.

Finally, the M.A.R.S club actively supports the CoRoDro team, which will present a collaborative project between a UAV and a Rover at the IGLUNA aerospace competition organized by Space Innovation this summer. This project requires many people to create and improve the software that will link the drone and the rover or to carry out tests to check the correct functioning of the equipment and captors but also to ensure the autonomy of decision of both of the drone and rover.

These three main areas of work animate the M.A.R.S. club and allows a real diversity inside the club. The students have the opportunity to work on concrete projects centered around the space theme.
